I belive we were still in grade school at the time...

We were rather young and dumb at the time. Looking for ways to keep ourselves from doing homework after school.
I had a big backyard, with plenty of open dirt to dig through. Me and him used to dig under rocks and rocks
to find the best earthworms, and then use them in earthworm races. It got rather intense, with the Thunderdome
being a shoebox lid filled with dirt. First worm to cross from one side to the other was the winner. What ensued
was lots of screaming, chanting, and overreactions. At that moment it felt like the fate of the world rested on
the backs of those worms. Between the two of us, I can't even remember who's worm won..

Silliness never leaves us

Back then it was common to just be silly to make each other laugh. We were just kids, and kids can be remarkably
strange and confusing. My purposefully unnamed friend is still a big part of my life, and that silliness lives on.
Adults are seen as more sensible and controlled, but that same childlike nonsense still lives on in our everyday life,
taking many shapes and forms. It could be something stupid you laugh at, or making noises to yourself in the shower because
you are bored. I personally think that sense of absurdity is what makes us human, and I hope it is something we never lose.
